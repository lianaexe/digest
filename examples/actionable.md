## What This Is

A detailed 3-day Tokyo itinerary covering major neighborhoods, food spots, and practical tips for first-time visitors.

## Action Items

- [ ] Book hotel in Shinjuku or Shibuya area (central, good transit access)
- [ ] Get Suica/Pasmo IC card at airport for all transit
- [ ] Day 1: Shibuya → Harajuku → Meiji Shrine → Omotesando → Shinjuku
- [ ] Day 2: Tsukiji Outer Market (breakfast) → TeamLab → Odaiba → Akihabara
- [ ] Day 3: Senso-ji Temple → Ueno Park → Ameyoko → Ginza → Tokyo Tower (sunset)
- [ ] Download Google Maps offline map for Tokyo
- [ ] Bring cash — many small restaurants are cash-only
- [ ] Reserve teamLab tickets online in advance (sells out)

## Details

**Transit**: JR Yamanote Line connects most major areas. A day pass is worth it if visiting 4+ stations. IC card works on all trains, buses, and many vending machines.

**Food tips**: Conveyor belt sushi for budget meals. Department store basement floors (depachika) have incredible takeaway food. Ramen streets in Tokyo Station are tourist-friendly with good quality.

**Budget**: Expect ~$100-150/day for food + transit + activities (excluding hotel).

**Timing**: Temples and shrines open early (6-7am) — go early to avoid crowds. Shopping areas come alive after 11am.

## Resources Mentioned

- teamLab Borderless — book at teamlab.art
- Suica card — available at JR ticket machines in airports
- Tabelog app — local restaurant ratings (more trusted than Google in Japan)
