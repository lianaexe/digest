## Summary

The article draws parallels between James Clear's Atomic Habits framework and software engineering practices. The author argues that small, consistent improvements to codebases — refactoring one function per PR, writing one additional test per feature — compound over time into dramatically better code quality. She shares data from her team's experiment: after 6 months of "atomic refactoring," their bug rate dropped and code review turnaround improved significantly.

## Key Points

- The "1% better every day" principle applies directly to codebases — tiny refactors compound
- "Habit stacking" for engineers: attach a small improvement to every PR (e.g., always add one test)
- Environment design matters — teams with linters and formatters auto-configured had higher adherence
- Identity-based habits: shifting from "I write code" to "I am someone who improves code quality"
